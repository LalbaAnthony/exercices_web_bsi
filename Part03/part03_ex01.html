Cats

Etymology

The origin of the English word 'cat', Old English catt, is thought to be the Late Latin word cattus, which was first used at the beginning of the 6th century.

Characteristics

Size

The domestic cat has a smaller skull and shorter bones than the European wildcat.

Skeleton

Cats have seven cervical vertebrae (as do most mammals); 13 thoracic vertebrae (humans have 12); seven lumbar vertebrae (humans have five); three sacral vertebrae (as do most mammals, but humans have five); and a variable number of caudal vertebrae in the tail (humans have only vestigial caudal vertebrae, fused into an internal coccyx).

Hunting and feeding

The shape and structure of cats' cheeks is insufficient to suck.

Free-fed feral cats and house cats consume several small meals in a day.
